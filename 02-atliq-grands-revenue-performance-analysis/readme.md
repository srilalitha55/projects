📊 AtliQ Grands Revenue Performance Analysis
🧾 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing revenue performance for AtliQ Grands, a luxury hotel chain in India, to identify key factors impacting revenue decline and market share loss.

The objective is to leverage data analytics and business intelligence to uncover insights related to occupancy, pricing, customer behavior, and booking patterns, enabling data-driven decision-making for revenue growth.

❗ Problem Statement

AtliQ Grands has been operating in the hospitality industry for over 20 years but is currently facing:

Declining market share in the luxury/business hotel segment
Revenue loss due to increased competition
Ineffective decision-making due to lack of data insights

The company lacks an in-house analytics team and wants to:

Understand revenue performance across cities and properties
Identify factors affecting occupancy and customer satisfaction
Improve strategic decision-making using data
🎯 Objective
Analyze hotel revenue performance across multiple dimensions
Track key hospitality KPIs like occupancy, ADR, and RevPAR
Identify trends and performance gaps
Provide actionable insights to improve revenue and market position
📌 Key Metrics

The dashboard focuses on the following KPIs:

Revenue
Occupancy %
Average Daily Rate (ADR)
Revenue Per Available Room (RevPAR)
Average Rating
Booking Cancellation Rate

📌 RevPAR is a critical metric as it combines both occupancy and pricing efficiency, reflecting overall revenue performance.

📊 Dashboard Features
KPI overview with week-over-week trend analysis
City-wise performance breakdown (Revenue, Occupancy, Ratings)
Property-level performance insights
Booking platform analysis (contribution %)
Day-type analysis (Weekday vs Weekend occupancy)
Trend analysis by weeks for key metrics
📁 Dataset Information

The project uses multiple datasets:

dim_date – Date details (day type, week number, month)
dim_hotels – Hotel/property information (city, category)
dim_rooms – Room classification details
fact_aggregated_bookings – Aggregated booking & capacity data
fact_bookings – Detailed booking transactions

📌 Dataset includes booking behavior, revenue metrics, ratings, and platform-level data for comprehensive analysis

⚠️ Data Disclaimer

Datasets used in this project are not included in this repository due to data privacy and usage guidelines.

However, the dashboard, insights, and analysis are fully based on the provided dataset structure and business problem.

💡 Key Insights
Revenue decline is primarily driven by lower occupancy in certain cities
Weekend occupancy is significantly higher than weekdays, indicating demand imbalance
Some booking platforms contribute more to revenue but also show higher cancellation rates
Lower-rated properties tend to have reduced occupancy and revenue performance
Certain room categories are underutilized, impacting overall RevPAR
🚀 Recommendations
Focus on improving weekday occupancy through targeted offers and pricing strategies
Optimize pricing (ADR) based on demand patterns and city performance
Strengthen partnerships with high-performing booking platforms
Improve service quality in low-rated properties to boost customer satisfaction
Optimize room inventory allocation to improve occupancy and revenue
